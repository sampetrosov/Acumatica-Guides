## Self-Service Portal Overview 

 The Acumatica Self-Service Portal is a site designed to give the employees of your company limited access to Acumatica ERP instance. 

 By using the Self-Service Portal, you can manage your contact and user information, place orders in Acumatica ERP, view shipments and all the relevant information about your interactions, create and view cases, and view financial reports. 

#### Your User Profile 

 In the Self-Service Portal, your personal information is stored, such as your name, organization, email address, phone number, and postal address. 

 You can view and edit this information any time you need to. You can also view information about your user account and change your password without contacting the portal system administrator. For details, see Your User Profile. 

#### User Interface 

 Employees in different positions use different sets of forms in the Self-Service Portal to perform their work tasks. 

 You can configure the user interface to have at hand the forms you frequently use. You can tailor the workspace menu items in the main menu so that the system displays only menu items related to your work. 

 For more information, see Personalizing the Interface. 

#### Your Company's Account 

 On the Company Profile (SP408030) form of the Self-Service Portal, you can update your company's address information, official website, and main contact details. You can also update the attention line to define a person or a department to whom emails or documents of your company are referred. For more information, see Your Company's Information. 

 To edit this information, you must be signed in to the Self-Service Portal with a user account that has the Customer Admin , Administrator , or Portal Admin role assigned to it. 

#### Online Ordering 

 In the Self-Service Portal, you can browse the catalog on the Catalog (SP700000) form. The catalog contains the list of items that you can add to your shopping cart. You can search for specific items, filter items by category, and show or hide the items that are not available in stock. You can also view detailed information about any item. If an item is stored in different warehouses, you can select the default warehouse you want the item to be shipped from. On this form, you can also modify the contents of the shopping cart by adding new items, deleting items, and changing the quantities of the added items. 

 On the My Cart (SP700001) form, you can review the items you plan to order, return to the Catalog form to continue shopping, or proceed to checkout. You can confirm the order by using the wizard pages on the Checkout (SP700002) form. 

 Aer you place an order, you can track the order and the order's shipments by using the My Orders (SP700003) form. You can also cancel the order, if needed. 

 For more information, see Managing Online Orders.

#### Finance Overview 

 On the My Documents (SP402000) form of the Self-Service Portal, you can view your company’s documents, such as invoices, credit memos, and payments. You can also view aging reports. 

 The visibility of these financial documents depends on the Display Financial Documents setting specified on the Portal Preferences (SP800000) form. You can access this form if you are signed in to the Self-Service Portal with a user account that has the Administrator or Portal Admin role assigned. 

 Depending on the Display Financial Documents option that has been selected, it may be possible to view documents associated with all companies and branches that are available for your company tenant, only a particular branch, or only a particular company. 

 In the Self-Service Portal, you can print or export these documents to a PDF or to Microso Excel. 

 For more information, see Your Company's Financial Documents. 

#### Case Management 

 In the Self-Service Portal, you can create support cases with the vendor if these capabilities have been configured in the system. Through cases, you can submit requests, complaints, and questions about items that you have bought or are considering buying. You can also retrieve the latest case updates, add information, attach files, and close or reopen cases. 

 For more information, see Case Management.

- You can change your password or reset it. For details, see _To Change Your Password_.

### Your Company's Contacts 

 In the Acumatica Self-Service Portal, customer contacts are the users of your company that have permission to access the system and work in it.

## Managing Customer Accounts 

 In the Acumatica Self-Service Portal, the employee of you vendor can create and manage a customer contact, which is linked to your company's business account. 

### Managing Customer Accounts 

 Acting as an employee of the company that uses the Acumatica ERP instance, in the Acumatica Self-Service Portal, you can use the Customer Account (SP408032) form to create a customer contact and link it to a business account. In the Self-Service Portal, you can open the Customer Account (SP408032) form by clicking a business account identifier on the Customer Accounts (SP408033) form. 

 Customer accounts are business accounts of companies that can access the Self-Service Portal. Customer contacts are the employees of your company who have permission to access to the Self-Service Portal and work in it. 

 For each customer account, you create a customer contact, which is linked to a company's business account. Then you enter contact information for the customer's employee, which can be the contact's name, position, and email address. 

 You can create a customer contact by signing in to the Self-Service Portal instance with a user account that has the Administrator role assigned. 

### To Create a Customer Contact

#### Signing In 

 To start using the Self-Service Portal, you need to sign in. To do so, perform these steps:

4. Check your email for further instructions. 

5. In the email, click the password recovery link.     This opens the Sign-In page for changing your password.

7. Click **Sign In**.

2. On the **Password recovery** screen, click **Forgot your username?**.

Your access rights to file attachments are determined by your rights to the entities to which the files are attached.

### Filters 

 In the Self-Service Portal, forms oen use tables to present data in ways that users can easily comprehend. But when a single table becomes several pages long, gleaning needed information from it becomes a challenge. Filters give you a means to display only those records that you want to see at the moment. 

 The Self-Service Portal has three types of filters, described in this section. The major difference in filter functionality is related to the way the filters can be used: 

- _Column Filters and Sorting_ (simple filters that are available on most form tables): Quickly add filters and     sorting to table columns. Such filters cannot be saved. 

- _Ad Hoc Filters_ (filters that are available on data entry forms): Create and apply complex filters when you     want to; such filters cannot be saved. 

- _Advanced Filters_ (filters that are available on processing forms): Create and apply complex filters, and save     them for future use. You can create such filters for your personal use and for sharing with other users. Also,     you can set an advanced filter as the default one. For details, see _Managing Advanced Filters_. For the description of the filtering by using the **Filter Settings** dialog box, see _Filter Settings Dialog Box_.
